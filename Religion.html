Hinduism



Hinduism is known as one of the world’s oldest religions, originating in India more than 4,000 years ago, and is currently the world’s third-largest religion. Hindus believe in a spiritual power called Brahman, the reason behind the existence of everything. Hindus also value one’s soul and believe in reincarnation. Hindus generally worship gods like Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Saraswathi, Lakshmi, Parvati, Durga, Ganesh, Hanuman, Krishna. In India, 79.8% of the population follows Hinduism.


Islam



Islam revolves around the belief in Allah and ideology present in the Quran. The Five Pillars of Islam are Profession of Faith (shahada), Prayer (salat), Alms (zakat), Fasting (saw) and Pilgrimage (hajj). They also believe that the righteous will be rewarded in heaven (Jannah), and those who commit wrong deeds will be punished in hell (Jahannam). Currently 14.2% of India's population follows Islam, and the biggest Islam festivals in India are Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha.


Jainism



Jainism is one of India’s ancient religions. Jainism teaches others to follow a path without violence (ahimsa), and to never harm any living being (including insects, animals, and plants). They also highly value truthfulness (satya). Jains largely follow Lord Mahavir, and similar to Hindus, they believe in karma and reincarnation. As of now, 0.45% of the country's population identifies as a Jain.


Sikhism



Sikhism originated from the Punjab region of India and was established by Guru Nanak. His ideologies were continued by nine other Gurus. Sikhs worship to god in a gurdwara, also known as a Sikh temple. Sikhism values equality for everyone and teaches its followers to work for the good of humanity, have honesty, and be devoted to God. In India, 1.72% of the population follows Sikhism.


Buddhism



Buddhism originated in India over 2,500 years ago and was established by Siddhartha Gautama. Since then, Buddhism is one of the largest religions in the world, especially in Asia. Buddhism encourages others to have the right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. Currently 0.7% of India's population follows Buddhism.


Christianity



Christianity started spreading in India from the southern region. Christianity is also a monotheistic religion, meaning they only follow one god. Christians base their beliefs off of the teachings that come from Jesus Christ's life and death. Christianity is known to spread to India through St. Thomas who came to India in 52 A.D. From this time period up till now, around 2.3% of Indians follow Christianity.


Minority religions



Judaism
Judaism is followed by Jewish people, and similar to Christianity and Islam, it spreads the idea that there is only one God that created the world. Judaism was one of the first foreign religions to spread in India.

Zoroastrian
Zoroastrianism is followed by those living in India with a Persian descendant, after they moved to India following the Muslim conquest of Persia (They are also known as Parsis)
