Taj Mahal, Agra



The Taj Mahal is located in the city of Agra (northern India). It is considered as one of the most beautiful architectures in the world and is recognized as one of the seven wonders of the world. The Taj Mahal was built by Shah Jahan, a Mughal emperor, in memory of his wife (Mumtaz Mahal). After her death in 1631, he began the construction for her tomb. The Taj Mahal is very special for India because it is recognized as India’s greatest achievement in architecture, due to the beautiful structure of the ivory-white marble mausoleum.

Gateway of India, Mumbai



The Gateway of India is the most unique monument in Mumbai, which represents the British rule and commemorated the 84,000 soldiers of the British India Army who died in war. Now the India gate is a war memorial site which is dedicated to all soldiers that sacrificed their life for India in war. It is a must-see spot for tourists.

The Golden Temple, Amritsar



The Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), is a gurudwara (Sikh temple) where Sikhs worship their god. It is located in the Amritsar, Punjab (Northwestern India). In India it is the most sacred place representing the Sikh religion, since it was where Guru Nanak lived.

The Red Fort, New Delhi



The Red Fort (also known as the Lal Qila) and is another one of India’s biggest architectural achievements. Its traditional style represented a fusion of Islamic, Hindu, and Persian architecture. The Red Fort is also a symbol of power for the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, and even represents India’s independence.

Amer Fort, Jaipur



Rajasthan is known for its beautiful architecture, with the Amer Fort in Jaipur taking the lead. The palace represents Indian royalty and its Rajput kings. Amer Fort is a monument from the Medieval period, in which the members of royalty lived in. The Sheesh Mahal (Glass Palace) at Amer Fort is a room that is surrounded by mirrors, which illuminates the whole palace with even a single ray of sunlight.

Ganges River, Varanasi



The Ganga River is the most famous and one of the longest rivers in India. It flows through Northern India and the Himalayas and all the way to Bangladesh. It not only has the function of helping agricultural regions with crop growth, but Hindus also consider it as the holiest river. They believe that submerging yourself in its water washes away your sins. This is why many Hindus go on difficult pilgrimages along the Ganges River.

Leh Ladakh



Ladakh is known for its beautiful landscapes with mountains and lakes making up its scenery. Ladakh is a perfect spot for those wanting to explore nature in India and want to go for trekking. For example, the Markha Valley trek goes through the beautiful Himalayan scenery.

The Golden City: Jaisalmer



Jaisalmer is known as the “Golden City” of India because of the yellow sandstone that is used throughout Jaisalmer. This is what makes Jaisalmer’s architecture so special and magnificent. The Jaisalmer Fort is situated on the Trikuta Mountains of Thar Desert and has been a part of many historic battles. In 2013, the Jaisalmer Fort was recognized as an UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Alappuzha, Kerala



Alappuzha is best known for its backwaters and beautiful rivers and lakes. A cruise along its canals will show you the magnificent nature of Kerela, while enjoying its authentic cuisine. The rivers of Alappuzha weave across half of Kerala. Alappuzha is also known for its boat races, temples, and beaches.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves, Aurangabad



These caves showcase Maharashtra’s greatest achievements in rock-cut architecture and lead you to temples and monasteries near Ajanta village. Those who visit these caves get astonished by its structure. These caves also celebrate the diverse religions of India since the Ellora caves are a mixture of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain, whereas the caves at Ajanta are purely Buddhist. Both these sites have been recognized as UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
